Why bitcoin could make finance worse.

Even if we disregard all of bitcoin's shortcomings, a financial instrument that brings out the worst in people—greed—won't change the world for the better.

Preamble.

I'm not necessarily against digital currencies. I've bought them, I've earned them, and I've used them before. However, after realizing the extent of its shady, dishonest, immoral, and spam-happy proponents, which makes it have more in common with a pyramid scheme than a reasonable investment, I chose to opt out. Am I salty? Definitely; I could've bought a house if I had been in a coma until now. So there's no point in denying that. Even worse, I'm experiencing cognitive dissonance because I know putting money in it could still be profitable, and because Tesla—the company that I initially invested in—just announced they bought bitcoin as well. “Fate loves irony” has come full circle.

Greed.
