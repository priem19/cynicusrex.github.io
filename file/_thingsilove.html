What I love.

Some stuff.

Professing a love for things as a minimalist might seem contradictory, but it needn't be. The essence of minimalism is reducing the stuff you own. E.g., don't buy something if you're barely going to use it; except for things like a fire extinguisher, naturally. For example, I hate that society encourages for everyone to have a car. They sit idle for X% of the time, they make the streets where they're parked look horrendous, and the traffic is both frustrating and deadly. No matter if they're gas or electric, too much is too much.
